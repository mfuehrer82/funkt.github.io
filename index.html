Eine Seite der PHP USERGROUP DRESDEN e.V.

Diversity is close to our hearts.

Posted on July 28, 2019

As a member of the PHP USERGROUP DRESDEN e.V. I can hardly put into words what my displeasure is about the current situation, which arose from the PHPCE CfP.

I was very interested to read the statement by Mark Baker and Larry Garfield skipping-php-ce-this-year. I know Mark Baker from the PHP Developer Days 2018 and I really appreciate his opinion. He is an experienced developer who knows the community as well. I understand that he has cancelled his participation in the conference due to the organizers' unwillingness to provide more diversity.

One of our goals as an association is, to support any person who is and wants to be part of the PHP community. In our Meetups and at our Conferences we try to open the doors to all members, regardless of their origin, gender or belonging to a minority, whether they are speakers or guests. We abhor discrimination and exclusion based on race, gender, sexual orientation or other characteristics.

“It wasn’t an easy decision to make, because I do enjoy sharing my coding passion; but having advocated for diversity at PHP developer conferences for the last several years, I have to follow my beliefs that diversity should be a cornerstone of the PHP developer community. Diversity matters more to me than speaking. “(Mark Baker)

Dear Mark, dear community, we as co-organizers, are very sorry that we did not manage to achieve more diversity. I myself was a part of the CfP voting. But instead of relying solely on the outcome of these votes, it would have been our job to uncover the reasons for the imbalance and, together with other organizational teams, to develop strategies to eliminate this imbalance. We are not the first to be faced with creating a balanced program within an unbalanced male domain. There are many good examples of conferences that have learned to solve these problems and open the doors to members of previously under-represented groups. We have failed so far and we are ready to learn from these role models.

First of all, I apologize on behalf of the PHP USERGROUP DRESDEN e.V. to everyone we have excluded because of our misconduct. We have failed to think in advance about what it means to hold a fair and open conference. We thought more about the location than about the community.

Second, I will contact the main organizers and try to find a solution with the help of the community. We don't want to post-nominate anyone just in order to satisfy a quota. Nevertheless, we would like to make up for a deeper mistake as soon as possible.

Thirdly, I will write to people who are working for more diversity in the community. I would also like to ask as many 'not only white men' as possible to become part of this year's conference. We would like to talk openly and honestly about the difficulties that arose during the creation of the Line-up. Let us use these mistakes as an opportunity to fight together in the future against the problem of lack of diversity.

For the future, we would like to improve in this area, and we would like to start as quickly as possible: With the help of the community, we want to cultivate an open, balanced list of speakers. We would like to invite these people to participate in CfPs in order to make future conferences more open and attractive for all. We will continue to offer Diversity Scholarships to enable all people in the community to participate in our conferences. We will hold a panel discussion on diversity during one of our conferences. In an open discussion with stakeholders, organizers and all other interested parties we will look at the problems from different perspectives. The goal is to find common ways to further promote diversity in the community and at conferences.

And any technical conference such as PHPCE is much more than simply a sharing of technical ideas. It is an event for socializing, and for networking. “(Mark Baker)

We want every participant of a conference to feel comfortable. Isn't it wonderful when people from different countries discuss the same problems and afterwards enjoy a cool drink together? A conference is always more than just sharing knowledge. A good conference creates space for everyone! We want nothing more than that.

Marcel Führer and the members of the PHP USERGROUP Dresden e.V.
